# n2uitive

> The leading recorded statement lifecycle management (RSLM) platform for P&C insurers

**Website:** [n2uitive.com](https://n2uitive.com/)

## Company Profile

- **Founded:** Unknown
- **Market position:** Market leader in recorded statements
- **Scale:** 25+ million statements processed
- **Customers:** 4 of Top 10 P&C insurers, Fortune 500 companies
- **Users:** 10,000+ adjusters daily, 40+ carriers/TPAs

## Product

**Core offering:** Recorded Statement Lifecycle Management (RSLM)

**Key features:**
- Mobile/web recording (audio-focused, not video)
- SummaryAssist™ — AI-generated summaries
- 1st Draft Transcript — searchable transcripts
- Guidewire ClaimCenter integration
- SAML 2.0 SSO
- Secure statement library with retention management
- One-click transcription ordering
- Real-time reporting for managers

## Pricing

Three tiers: Basic, Pro, Premium
- No public pricing — "contact for quote"
- Likely enterprise pricing model

## Positioning

**Tagline:** "Turn Recorded Statements into Claims Intelligence"

**Key messages:**
- "Purpose-built for recorded statements"
- "Save up to 75% on transcription costs"
- "Deploy in less than a week, train in 30 minutes"
- "99% customer satisfaction"

**Target:** Enterprise P&C insurers, claims managers, TPAs

## Strengths

- Massive market proof (25M statements)
- Enterprise credibility (Top 10 insurers)
- Guidewire integration (industry standard)
- AI summarization
- Established sales/support infrastructure

## Weaknesses

- **Audio-focused, not video** — ClaimMentor differentiator
- Enterprise pricing likely expensive for smaller carriers
- No visible Workers Comp specialization
- Generic claims positioning (not fraud/SIU focused)

## Competitive Strategy

1. **Don't compete on scale** — we can't claim 25M statements
2. **Compete on video** — they don't have it, we do
3. **Compete on transparency** — clear pricing vs. enterprise sales process
4. **Compete on specialization** — Workers Comp, fraud detection, SIU
5. **Compete on simplicity** — faster to deploy, easier to use
