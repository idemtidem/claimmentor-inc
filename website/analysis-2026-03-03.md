## Phase 2: Parallel Analysis

### Sub-Agent 2: Product Strategist

**Problem:** Current messaging is generic and feature-focused.

"Automates scheduling, recording, and summaries" could describe any meeting tool. It doesn't speak to insurance-specific problems.

**Recommendation:** Lead with insurance-specific outcomes, not features.

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Automates scheduling" | "End the 3-day phone tag to schedule statements" |
| "Recording" | "Video evidence for SIU and surveillance" |
| "Summaries" | "Catch morphing injuries with searchable transcripts" |
| "Better outcomes" | "Stronger litigation defense, lower claim costs" |

**Positioning opportunity:** n2uitive doesn't emphasize VIDEO. They're phone-recording focused. ClaimMentor's video-first approach is a differentiator for:
- Surveillance identification
- Seeing injury scenes
- Humanizing the claims process

---

### Sub-Agent 3: Customer Success (Voice of Customer)

**What Farm Bureau actually said they'd buy for:**

1. **Fraud detection via video** — "Surveillance doesn't have value if you cannot identify the worker."
2. **Morphing injury protection** — "Roll back the tape" when claims change over time
3. **Litigation support** — Facts on record for attorney negotiations
4. **Adjuster coaching** — Transcripts help managers train staff

**Missing from website:**

- No mention of Workers Comp (their segment)
- No mention of fraud/SIU
- No mention of litigation
- No mention of video specifically (vs. audio)
- No testimonials or use cases

**Language to steal from the customer:**
- "Roll back the tape"
- "Facts on record"
- "Identify the worker"
- "Taking statements is a skill"

### Sub-Agent 4: Market Analyst

## Phase 3: Executive Synthesizer

### Top 5 Recommendations

#### 1. Lead with VIDEO as the differentiator

**Current:** "Recording" (generic)
**Proposed:** "Video statements that catch fraud"

n2uitive is audio-focused. Video enables:
- SIU surveillance identification
- Seeing injury scenes
- Claimant credibility assessment
- Humanized claims experience

**Headline option:**
> "See Who You're Paying. Video Recorded Statements for Workers Comp."

---

#### 2. Speak to outcomes, not features

**Current:** "Automates scheduling, recording, and summaries"

**Proposed:** Address the problems they actually have:

| Problem | Outcome Message |
|---------|-----------------|
| Can't ID claimants for surveillance | "Put a face to every claim" |
| Injuries morph over time | "Roll back the tape when stories change" |
| Litigated claims cost money | "Facts on record for stronger defense" |
| Scheduling takes 3 days of calls | "Self-service scheduling, no phone tag" |

---

#### 3. Add Workers Comp focus

Farm Bureau is WC. That's a clear ICP (ideal customer profile).

**Add a WC-specific section or page:**
- Morphing injuries
- PTD fraud detection
- Employer/policyholder video evidence
- Spanish language support

---

#### 4. Build credibility fast

n2uitive has "4 of top 10 insurers" and "25M statements."

**ClaimMentor needs:**
- Customer logos (even 1-2)
- Specific numbers ("X% faster scheduling")
- Case study or testimonial
- "Built by insurance technology veterans" (from the demo — use it!)

---

#### 5. Simplify the CTA

**Current:** Unknown
**Proposed:** "See it in 15 minutes" or "Book a Demo"

Make it low-commitment. Farm Bureau said they'd need a couple weeks — don't scare them with "Start Free Trial" when they need procurement approval.
